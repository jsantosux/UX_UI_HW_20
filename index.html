Pals UCF Card
Non-Profit Website Redesign

PALS aspires to become the leading non-profit organization in Central Florida by providing evidence-based support, services and programs for individuals with autism spectrum disorders (ASD) and to create opportunities for individuals, of all ages, with ASD.

Project Overview


Challenge

PALS website isn’t meeting the usability needs for individuals with ASD which is causing confusion and stress while trying to navigate the site's content.


Solution

Improve the overall functionality of this site so those affected by this condition are successfully able to locate the information they require.

Role

UX UI Designer


Time

3 Weeks


Task

Responsive Web Redesign


Tools

Adobe XD, Figma, Miro, Google Suite


Design Process


empathize

Research


To empathize with our potential users, the first step in our design process was to gain a deeper understanding through user interviews. We set up a interview plan and interviewed 5 people for about 15-20 minutes each. Asking open ended questions allowed us to learn as much as we could about their experiences and identify what the users needs truly are.

Some questions asked during the interview:


  • What do know about Autism?
  • What could make PALS site simpler to use?
  • What areas on this site currently stand out to you that need to be fixed?

We were able to take the information gathered via user interviews and develop a cohesive affinity map.



User Persona

Through research, we were able to get a clear picture of who PAL's target users are and created a persona that would accurately represent them - meet Molly! This persona continued to guide our decisions moving forward, making sure our design is centered on who our user Molly is.



define + ideate

Defining the Problem


Now that we identified our target user, Molly, it was time to identify what actual problems we are solving for based on what we have learned about our user.

To help define these problems, we used the insights gained from research and our understanding of Molly’s needs to create a point of view statement to help frame the problem from the user’s perspective. We used this POV Statement to identify our How Might We question which would fuel our process to brainstorm solutions.

Molly needs to get connected with any ASD resources available because she often feels alone in her journey and wants to bridge the gap of social acceptance by building community.

How might we improve the overall functionality of this site so those affected by this condition are successfully able to locate the information they require?



Brainstorming

Now that we knew what problems we needed to solve for Molly, we started our brainstorming process to come up with solutions to those problems. Focusing on our HMW question helped identify and brainstorm different ideas.

I Like, I Wish, What If + Feature Prioritization Matrix



Information Architecture

Site Map

Focusing on the prioritized solutions, we created a site map to help define the overall structure of the content on PAL's website in a way that would be logical and easy to navigate for our user.

prototype & test

Building a Prototype to Test


Taking what we learned throughout this process, we started to make decisions on how the content on the PAL's website would be organized based on the project goals we wanted to meet.



Mid-Fidelity Wireframes

Taking our wireframe sketches, we digitized them in Adobe and added just enough information for users to be able to navigate through the pages and complete tasks that would be presented to them during usability testing. These mid-fidelity wireframes would help us focus on what needed to be improved in terms of the functionality of the design. A mobile version was also created to make sure that the design is responsive and effective across the different device screens that users would be accessing the website on.





Prototype


Usability Testing

With our prototype completed, we started working on a test plan to guide the testing that would be conducted. We then recruited participants and conducted remote guerrilla testing in order to see how users interact with the design and identified where improvements to the design could be made.

Test Objectives
  • Test if users can easily complete the tasks
  • Observe the different paths users take to complete tasks
  • Assess areas of improvement to improve the usability of the design

Tasks
  • Learn more about Autism on the PAL's site
  • Find out more about the programs available
  • See what kind of event's they have to offer
  • Takes the steps towards donating to this organization
